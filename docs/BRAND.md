# TrailKit Brand System

## Name

TrailKit

## Tagline

Track every app growth link from click to install to revenue.

## Brand Story

Every app team leaves trails across the internet: creator links, affiliate links, emails, QR codes, app buttons, campaign pages, and referral partners. TrailKit turns those trails into accountable growth paths by connecting each link to installs, revenue, and payout estimates.

## Voice

- Clear, direct, and technical enough to be trusted.
- Founder-friendly and practical.
- Honest about attribution limits.
- Tactical over abstract.
- Calm, precise, and helpful.

## Color Palette

- Deep ink: `#05070D` for primary background.
- Elevated ink: `#0B1020` and `#10182D` for panels.
- Electric cyan: `#2FD4FF` for primary actions and click signals.
- Blue: `#0EA5E9` for active states.
- Mint: `#55F0B0` for installs, success, and matched events.
- Amber: `#FFC766` for revenue and payouts.
- Soft text: `#EEF6FF`.
- Muted text: `#9AA8BD`.
- Border: `rgba(154, 168, 189, 0.18)`.

## Typography

Use Geist Sans and Geist Mono through `next/font/google`. The wordmark uses a high-weight system font treatment rather than a paid or custom font file.

## Logo Concept

A trail line with three connected nodes: click, install, and revenue. The path curves upward to suggest growth while staying simple enough to work as a favicon.

## Icon Concept

Use the three-node path mark for app icons, favicons, and product avatars. Icons in the UI should come from Lucide and use cyan, mint, or amber only for meaningful signal.

## UI Style

- Deep ink backgrounds.
- Thin grid lines.
- Glassy but restrained panels.
- 8px card radius.
- Compact dashboard surfaces.
- Strong metric hierarchy.
- Node-link diagrams for attribution paths.
- Avoid one-note blue/purple palettes.

## Visual Motifs

- Trails.
- Nodes.
- Link paths.
- Conversion funnels.
- Store routing.
- Revenue pulses.
- Payout ledgers.

## Landing Page Art Direction

Use a dark infrastructure-style page with a first-viewport product visual. The hero should show clicks turning into installs, trials, revenue, and payouts. Keep copy short and specific. The page should feel like a useful product, not a generic startup template.

## Do

- Say "estimate" and "match" when attribution may be incomplete.
- Show product UI early.
- Use concrete channel examples: creators, affiliates, emails, QR codes, website buttons, referral partners.
- Keep product screens dense and readable.

## Don't

- Promise perfect tracking.
- Mention bypassing ATT or fingerprinting.
- Lead with creator CRM language.
- Make RevenueCat the only integration path.
- Use oversized marketing cards inside the app dashboard.
